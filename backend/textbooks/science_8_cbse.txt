Crop Production and Management:
Plants of the same kind grown and cultivated at one place on a large scale are called a crop.
Kharif Crops: Sown in rainy season (June to Sept). Example: Paddy, Maize.
Rabi Crops: Sown in winter season (Oct to March). Example: Wheat, Gram.
Basic practices of crop production: Preparation of soil, Sowing, Adding manure and fertilisers, Irrigation, Protecting from weeds, Harvesting, Storage.

Microorganisms: Friend and Foe.
Microorganisms are classified into four major groups: Bacteria, Fungi, Protozoa, and Algae.
Viruses are also microscopic but are different from other microorganisms.
Lactobacillus is a bacterium that helps in the formation of curd.
Antibiotics are medicines that kill or stop the growth of disease-causing microorganisms.
Penicillin was discovered by Alexander Fleming in 1929.
